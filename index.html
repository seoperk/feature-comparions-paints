Feature Comparison Among Paint 3D, Crello, and MS Paint

A detailed 2026 analysis of three popular design and editing tools

Introduction

In the evolving world of digital design and image editing in 2026, choosing the right tool can significantly impact your creative workflow. This article compares three accessible options: Paint 3D, Crello (now rebranded as VistaCreate), and the classic MS Paint. Each serves different needs—from basic sketching to professional graphic design and 3D modeling. Paint 3D, introduced by Microsoft, blends 2D editing with 3D capabilities, making it ideal for beginners exploring dimensionality. Crello, a template-driven graphic design platform, excels in quick social media and marketing visuals. MS Paint, the timeless Windows staple, offers simplicity for fast edits. Understanding their differences ensures you pick the tool that enhances productivity without unnecessary complexity.

Overview of Each Tool

Paint 3D

Launched with Windows 10's Creators Update, Paint 3D extends MS Paint into three dimensions. It supports 2D drawing with advanced brushes, textures, and effects, plus 3D modeling tools like primitives, doodling, and object manipulation. Features include Magic Select for easy cutouts, stickers, and 3D text. Though deprecated from the Microsoft Store in 2024, it's still downloadable and functional on Windows 11 in 2026. Its integration with Remix 3D (legacy) and export options make it versatile for casual creators.

Crello (VistaCreate)

Crello, rebranded to VistaCreate in 2021, is an online graphic design tool focused on templates for social media, print, and web. It offers thousands of customizable designs, stock images, animations, and video editing. With drag-and-drop simplicity, it's beginner-friendly for marketing materials. Premium features unlock more assets, but the free version suffices for basic needs. Unlike paint programs, it's more about composition than freehand drawing.

MS Paint

The original Microsoft Paint, updated in Windows 11 with layers and dark mode by 2026, remains a lightweight 2D editor. It includes basic brushes, shapes, text, and color tools. Ideal for quick annotations or simple illustrations, it loads instantly and requires no learning curve. Recent enhancements add transparency and autosave, but it lacks 3D or advanced design features.

Pros and Cons

Paint 3D Pros & Cons

Pros: Versatile 3D tools, realistic painting, free and integrated with Windows. Cons: Deprecated status may limit updates, steeper learning curve for 3D, no cloud collaboration.

Crello (VistaCreate) Pros & Cons

Pros: Vast template library, animation capabilities, user-friendly for non-designers. Cons: Requires internet, premium features locked behind paywall, less focus on freehand art.

MS Paint Pros & Cons

Pros: Extremely fast, simple interface, no installation needed on Windows. Cons: Limited features, no 3D or templates, basic output quality.

Use Cases

For hobbyists experimenting with 3D: Paint 3D shines in creating mixed 2D/3D art, like doodling models or texturing objects. Social media managers: Crello's templates speed up post creation with animations and stock elements. Quick edits: MS Paint is unbeatable for annotating screenshots or simple memes.

In education, Paint 3D aids STEM projects with 3D visualization. Businesses use Crello for marketing collaterals. Casual users stick with MS Paint for everyday tweaks. Each tool's strengths align with specific scenarios, making hybrid workflows common.

Conclusion

In 2026, Paint 3D offers the best balance for creative exploration with 3D, Crello excels in professional design efficiency, and MS Paint remains the go-to for simplicity.
